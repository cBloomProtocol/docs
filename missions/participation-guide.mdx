Participating in social missions is simple: post on X with the right elements, and Bloom Protocol handles the rest.

## The Three Must-Haves

Every social mission submission requires three essential elements. Miss any one, and your submission won't be detected or approved.

### 1. Mission Account Mention

<AccordionGroup>
<Accordion title="@bloom_mission">

**What**: Include @bloom_mission in your post

**Why**: This is how our system discovers your submission when scanning X

**Format**: Type it exactly as `@bloom_mission` (lowercase, with underscore)

**Example Post**:
```
Just completed the awesome challenge from @ProjectTeam!
Here's my detailed feedback ðŸ‘‡
@bloom_mission
```

<Warning>
**Common mistakes**:
- Writing `@bloom-mission` (dash instead of underscore)
- Writing `@bloomprotocol` (different account)
- Forgetting to include it entirely
</Warning>

</Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

### 2. Project Team Mention

<AccordionGroup>
<Accordion title="Dev Team Account">

**What**: Mention the project's X account specified in the mission details

**Why**: Connects your submission to the specific mission and notifies the project team

**Where to find it**: Look for "Dev Team" or "Project Account" in the mission details page

**Example from mission page**:
```
Mission by: Seedify (@SeedifyFund)
You must mention: @SeedifyFund
```

**Example Post**:
```
Tested @SeedifyFund's new launchpad feature.
The UX is incredibly smooth! Full review: [link]
@bloom_mission bloomprotocol.ai/alice
```

<Note>
Different missions have different project accounts. Always check the mission details for the correct account to mention.
</Note>

</Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

<AccordionGroup>

## Can I Submit Multiple Times?

**Yes! You can improve your submission by end of time.**

This is one of the best features of social missions - you're not locked into your first attempt.

### How Replacement Works

<Steps>
  <Step title="Post #1: First Attempt">
    You submit your initial post. System creates submission #1.
  </Step>

  <Step title="You Realize You Can Do Better">
    Maybe you found more insights, created better content, or got more thorough feedback.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Post #2: Improved Version">
    You post again with all required elements. System detects it.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Automatic Replacement">
    System sees it's the same user (UID), same Twitter account, different post.
    Submission #1 is replaced by submission #2.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Old Post Archived">
    Your first post is marked as "replaced by [new post]" in the system (audit trail).
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Replacement Rules

| Condition | Required? | Why |
|-----------|-----------|-----|
| Same UID | âœ“ Yes | Must be your profile link |
| Same Twitter account | âœ“ Yes | Can't switch accounts |
| Different post | âœ“ Yes | Must be a new post |
| Same mission | âœ“ Yes | Replaces within that mission only |
| Before deadline | âœ“ Yes | Can't replace after mission ends |

<Check>**You can**: Submit as many improved versions as you want before deadline</Check>
<Check>**You can**: Replace even after initial approval (if deadline hasn't passed)</Check>
<Check>**System automatically**: Keeps only your latest submission</Check>

<Warning>
**You cannot**:
- Submit from multiple Twitter accounts for the same mission
- Use different profile links (UIDs) for the same mission
- Replace after the mission deadline has passed
- Submit the same exact post URL multiple times
</Warning>

### Why This Matters

> **Quality is encouraged, not punished**
>
> The replacement feature means:
> - No penalty for experimenting
> - Iterate based on what you learn
> - Submit when you're proud of it, not when you're rushed
> - Your best work counts, not your first work
